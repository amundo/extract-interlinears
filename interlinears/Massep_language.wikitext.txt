'''Massep''' (Masep, Potafa, Wotaf) is a poorly documented [[Papuan languages|Papuan language]] spoken by fewer than 50 people in the single village of Masep in [[:id:Pantai Barat, Sarmi|West Pantai District]], [[Sarmi Regency]], [[Papua (Indonesian province)|Papua]]. Despite the small number of speakers, however, language use is vigorous. It is surrounded by the [[Kwerba languages]] [[Airoran language|Airoran]] and [[Samarokena language|Samarokena]].<ref name="Foley-NWNG"/>

==Classification==
Clouse, Donohue, and Ma (2002) conclude that it definitely is not a [[Kwerba languages|Kwerba language]],<ref name="Clouse-2002">{{cite journal|url=http://www-01.sil.org/silesr/2002/SILESR2002-078.pdf|title=Survey Report of the North Coast of Irian Jaya|last1=Clouse|first1=Duane|last2=Donohue|first2=Mark|last3=Ma|first3=Felix|year=2002|website=SIL|access-date=2018-07-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303200905/http://www-01.sil.org/silesr/2002/SILESR2002-078.pdf|archive-date=2016-03-03|url-status=dead}}</ref> as it had been classified by Wurm (1975). They did not notice connections to any other language family. However, Usher (2018) classifies it as Greater Kwerbic.<ref>Usher, Timothy. [https://sites.google.com/site/newguineaworld/families/north-new-guinea/foja-range/west-foja-range West Foja Range]. ''New Guinea World''.</ref>

''Ethnologue'', ''Glottolog'', and Foley (2018)<ref name="Foley-NWNG"/> list it as a [[language isolate]],<ref name=e18/><ref>{{cite web| editor-last1= Hammarström| editor-first1 = Harald| editor-last2 = Forke| editor-first2 = Robert| editor-last3 = Haspelmath| editor-first3 = Martin| editor-last4 = Bank| editor-first4 = Sebastian| year = 2020|title = Massep  | work = [[Glottolog]] 4.3| url = https://glottolog.org/resource/languoid/id/mass1263}}</ref> but it has not been included in wider surveys, such as Ross (2005). The pronouns are not dissimilar from those of [[Trans–New Guinea languages]], but Massep is geographically distant from that family.
