'''Ngkolmpu Kanum''', or '''Ngkontar''', is part of a dialect chain in the Yam family spoken by the [[Kanum people]] of [[New Guinea]]. The Ngkâlmpw (Ngkontar) and [[moribund language|moribund]] Bädi varieties have limited [[mutual intelligibility]] may be considered distinct languages.<ref name="Evans-Southern">{{cite book |last=Evans |first=Nicholas |authorlink=Nicholas Evans (linguist) |editor1-last=Palmer |editor1-first=Bill |date=2018 |title=The Languages and Linguistics of the New Guinea Area: A Comprehensive Guide |chapter=The languages of Southern New Guinea |series= The World of Linguistics |volume=4 |location=Berlin |publisher=De Gruyter Mouton |pages=641–774 |isbn=978-3-11-028642-7}}</ref>

== Dialects ==
Languages spoken by the Kanum have variously been referred to as Ngkâlmpw Kanum, Enkelembu, Kenume, and Knwne.<ref>{{Glottolog|kanu1280}}</ref><ref name="Carroll 2016">{{Cite thesis |last=Carroll |first=Matthew J. |title=The Ngkolmpu Language with special reference to distributed exponence |date=2016 |degree=PhD |publisher=The Australian National University |url=https://openresearch-repository.anu.edu.au/handle/1885/116801 |doi=10.25911/5D74E0CFD5B85  |doi-access=free }}</ref> Carroll describes three varieties forming a dialect chain. Ngkolmpu is divided into Ngkontar and the moribund variety Baedi (Bädi).

== Phonology ==

=== Consonants ===
Ngkolmpu Kanum has 15 consonant phonemes (plus two marginal phonemes) at three points of articulation: bilabial, coronal, and velar. Prenasalized voiceless stops and fricatives contrast with voiceless and nasal realizations, which is typologically unusual. The orthography is enclosed in angle brackets.

== Grammar ==
The Ngkolmpu (Ngkâlmpw) Kanum variety is notable for its complex verbal inflection and tendency to distribute grammatical features throughout an utterance, referred to as distributed exponence.<ref name="Carroll 2016" />
