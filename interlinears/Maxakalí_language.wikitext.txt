'''Maxakalí''' (''Tikmũũn yĩy ax'', ''Mãxakani yĩy ax''<ref name="Silva-2020" />{{rp|22}}) is a [[Maxakalían|Maxakalían language]] spoken in four villages in [[Minas Gerais]], [[Brazil]], by more than 2,000 people.<ref name="Silva-2020">{{Cite thesis |last=Silva |first=Mário André Coelho da |title=Tikmũũn yĩy ax tinã xohi xi xahĩnãg – Sons e pedaços da língua Maxakalí: descrição da fonologia e morfologia de uma língua Macro-Jê |date=2020 |degree=Ph.D. |publisher=Universidade Federal de Minas Gerais |language=pt |hdl=1843/33613 |hdl-access=free}}</ref>{{rp|30}}

==Dialects==
No dialectal differences are known. Extinct varieties such as [[Kapoxó language|Kapoxó]], Kumanaxó, [[Makuní language|Makuní]], Panháme, and the 19th century "Maxakalí", which were sometimes taken to be dialects of Maxakalí, are now generally considered to represent a distinct variety of the [[Maxakalían|Maxakalían family]], very close to [[Ritual Maxakalí language|Ritual Maxakalí]].<ref name="Silva-2020" />{{rp|39–42}} [[Curt Nimuendaju]] collected a wordlist of a variety known as ''Mašakarí/Monačóbm'' in 1939, which was shown by Araújo (1996) to be an early attestion of Maxakalí.<ref name="Araújo-1996">{{Cite journal |last=Araújo |first=Gabriel Antunes de |date=1996 |title=Cadernos de Estudos Lingüísticos |url=http://etnolinguistica.wdfiles.com/local--files/artigo%3Aaraujo-1996/araujo_1996_masakari.pdf |journal=Cadernos de Estudos Lingüísticos |language=pt |volume=31 |pages=5–31}}</ref>

Spoken Maxakalí is different from the variety used in the Maxakalí ritual songs, [[Ritual Maxakalí]], though both are classified as [[Maxakalían]] languages.

==Distribution==
Maxakalí was originally spoken in the [[Mucuri River]], [[Itanhém River]], and [[Jequitinhonha River]] areas. Today, Maxakalí is found in four main communities (''aldeias'') of [[Minas Gerais]], with a total ethnic population of about 2,000:<ref name="Silva-2020" />{{rp|30}}

*Pradinho (Maxakalí name: ''Pananiy''), in [[Bertópolis]], Minas Gerais
*Água Boa (Maxakalí name: ''Kõnãg Mai'' or ''Akmamo''), in [[Santa Helena de Minas]], Minas Gerais
*Aldeia Verde (Maxakalí name: ''Apne Yĩxux''), in [[Ladainha, Minas Gerais|Ladainha]], Minas Gerais
*Cachoeirinha (Maxakalí name: ''Ĩmmoknãg''), in [[Teófilo Otoni]], Minas Gerais

'''Old Machacari''' is attested from the 19th century. Reported varieties include Monoxó, Makoni, Kapoxó, Kumanaxó, and Panhame. After the dispersion of its speakers in the 1750s, they lived between the upper [[Mucuri River]] and [[São Mateus River]] (near the present-day city of [[Teófilo Otoni]], Minas Gerais), possibly up to [[Jequitinhonha]] in the north to the [[Suaçuí Grande River]], a tributary of the [[Doce River]], in the south. After 1750, the southward migration of the [[Botocudo]]s forced the Machacari to seek refuge in Portuguese settlements on the Atlantic coast (in an area ranging from the mouth of the [[Mucuri River]] to the [[Itanhaém River]]), in [[Alto dos Bois]] (near [[Minas Novas]]), and in [[Peçanha]].<ref name="Ramirez" /> According to Saint-Hilaire (2000: 170),<ref>{{Cite book |last=Saint-Hilaire |first=Auguste de |title=Viagem pelas províncias do Rio de Janeiro e Minas Gerais |date=2000 |publisher=Editora Itatiaia |location=Belo Horizonte |language=pt}}</ref> the Monoxó lived in [[Cuyaté]] ([[Doce River]], near the mouth of the [[Suaçuí Grande River]]) probably around 1800, before seeking refuge in Peçanha.<ref name="Ramirez">{{Cite journal |last=Ramirez |first=Henri |last2=Vegini |first2=Valdir |last3=França |first3=Maria Cristina Victorino de |date=2015 |title=Koropó, puri, kamakã e outras línguas do Leste Brasileiro |journal=LIAMES: Línguas Indígenas Americanas |language=pt |volume=15 |issue=2 |pages=223–277 |doi=10.20396/liames.v15i2.8642302 |doi-access=free}}</ref> At the beginning of the 19th century, the Panhame and other Maxakali groups allied with the Portuguese to fight the Botocudos.<ref name="Ramirez" />

'''Modern Maxakali''' (called '''Monaxobm''' by [[Curt Nimuendajú]]) is distinct from Old Machacari. It was historically spoken from the [[Mucuri River]] valley up to the headwaters of the [[Itanhaém River]] in [[Minas Gerais]].<ref name="Ramirez" />

Silva (2020) describes two nasal spread processes which affect vowels.<ref name="Silva-2020" />

====Vowel lowering====
According to Silva (2020), all vowels except /a ã/ have lowered allophones.<ref name="Silva-2020" />{{rp|102}}

The vowels /ɛ ɛ̃ i ĩ ɨ ɨ̃ u ũ/ are lowered to [æ æ̃ ɪ ɪ̃ ɨ ɨ̃ ʊ ʊ̃], respectively, preceding a palatal coda. Examples include ''tex ~ tehex'' [ˈt̪æj ~ t̪æˈɦæj] ‘rain’, ''yẽy'' [ˈɲæ̃j] ‘to shut up, to be silent’, ''pix'' [ˈpɪj] ‘wash (''realis'')’, ''mĩy'' [ˈmɪ̃j] ‘make (''realis'')’, ''kux'' [ˈkɨ̞j] ‘to end; forehead’, ''mũy'' [ˈmɨ̞̃j̃] ‘to hold, to grab (''irrealis'')’, ''tox'' [ˈt̪ʊj ~ ˈt̪uwɪ] ‘long’, ''nõy'' [ˈn̪ʊ̃j] ‘other; same-sex sibling’.

The vowels /ɨ ɨ̃ u ũ/ are further lowered to [ɘ ɘ̃ o õ], respectively, preceding a velar coda, as in ''tuk'' [ˈt̪ɘɰ] ‘to grow up’, ''yũmũg'' [ɲɨˈ̃mɘ̃ɰ̃] ‘to know, to understand, to learn’, ''ponok'' [puˈd̪oɰ] ‘white’, ''mõg'' [ˈmõɰ̃] ‘to go (''realis'')’. The front vowels /ɛ ɛ̃ i ĩ/ are never followed by a surface velar coda, because underlying velar codas are palatalized to palatal codas in this environment.

In addition, /ɨ̃/ surfaces as [ɘ̃] word-finally, as in ''yõgnũ'' [ɲõɰ̃ŋ̞̊ˈn̪ɘ̃ʔ] ‘it is mine’, ''xõnnũ'' [ʨũːˈn̪ɘ̃ʔ] ‘son! (''vocative'')’, ''nũ'' [ˈn̪ɘ̃ʔ] ‘this; to come (''irrealis'').

====Backing of /a ã/====
The vowels /a ã/ are backed to [ɑ ɑ̃] preceding a coronal (dental or palatal) coda.<ref name="Silva-2020" />{{rp|109–10}} Examples include ''put(ah)at'' [pɨˈt̪(ɑɦ)ɑə̯] ‘road’, ''n(ãh)ãn'' [ˈn̪(ɑ̃ɦ)ɑ̃ə̯̃] ‘achiote’, ''hax'' [ˈhɑj] ‘smell, to smell’, ''gãx'' [ˈɡɑ̃j] ‘angry’.<ref name="Silva-2020" />{{rp|109–10}}

The vowels /a ã/ are backed and rounded in open syllables following a labial onset, as in ''kopa'' [kuˈpɒʔ] ‘inside’, ''hõmã'' [hũˈmɒ̃ʔ] ‘long ago’.<ref name="Silva-2020" />{{rp|110}}

The nasals [m n̪ ɲ] have been analyzed as allophones of /b d̪ dʑ/ preceding nasal nuclei, but the contrast between /m n̪ ɲ/ and /b d̪ dʑ/ is emerging in Portuguese borrowings and in diminutives.<ref name="Silva-2020" />{{rp|155–63}}

In the coda position, only the place of articulation is contrastive, the possibilities being labial (orthographic ''-p ~ -m''), dental (''-t ~ -n''), palatal (''-x ~ -y''), and velar (''-k ~ -g''). The typical realization of the codas involves prevocalization, the consonantal element itself being optional.<ref name="Silva-2020" />

===Absence of fricatives and nasals===
The World Atlas of Language Structures claims that Maxakalí has no contrastive fricative or nasal consonants, citing "Gudschinski et al. 1970".<ref>{{Cite web |title=Language Maxakalí |url=https://wals.info/languoid/lect/wals_code_max |website=WALS Online |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal |last=Gudschinsky |first=Sarah C. |last2=Popovich |first2=Harold |last3=Popovich |first3=Frances |date=1970 |title=Native Reaction and Phonetic Similarity in Maxakalí Phonology |journal=Language |language=en |volume=46 |issue=1 |pages=77–88 |doi=10.2307/412408 |jstor=412408}}</ref> It is important to note that WALS did not consider [h] to be a true fricative in this judgement. The phonological status of the nasal consonants is ambiguous; Silva (2020) argues that in modern Maxakalí they are becoming contrastive through phonologization, even though until recently nasal consonants occurred only as allophones of voiced obstruents.<ref name="Silva-2020" />{{rp|155–63}}

==Syntax==

===Word order===
The most common word order in Maxakalí is SOV.

{{interlinear|indent=3|glossing=link
|Kakxop te xok-hep xo’op
|child ERG animal-liquid drink
|"The child drinks milk"}}

===Pronominal forms and morphosyntactic alignment===
Most clause types in Maxakalí are characterized by the ergative–absolutive morphosyntactic alignment. The agents of transitive verbs are marked by the [[ergative case|ergative]] postposition ''te'', whereas the patients of transitive verbs and the intransitive subjects are unmarked. Absolutive pronominal participants are expressed by person prefixes; ergative pronominal participants take special forms upon receiving the ergative postposition ''te''. The same forms are found with other postpositions; furthermore, ''ã'' and ''xa'' occur as the irregular inflected forms of the dative postposition ''pu'' in the first person singular and in the second person, respectively.

==Morphology==
===Mood inflection===
Maxakalí verbs inflect for mood. The [[realis mood]] is the most common one, whereas the [[irrealis mood]] is used in imperative and purpose clauses. The morphological exponence of the mood inflection follows one of at least 7 patterns.<ref>{{Cite book |last=Silva |first=Mário André Coelho da |url=http://araguaia2.ufmt.br/ebook/ebook1.pdf |title=Línguas e culturas Macro-Jê: saberes entrecruzados |last2=Nikulin |first2=Andrey |date=2020 |publisher=GEDDELI/Universidade Federal de Mato Grosso |isbn=978-65-00-02975-8 |editor-last=Miranda |editor-first=Maxwell |location=Barra do Garças |pages=329–351 |language=pt |chapter=Morfologia verbal flexional da língua Maxakalí |editor-last2=Borges |editor-first2=Águeda Aparecida da Cruz |editor-last3=Santana |editor-first3=Áurea Cavalcante |editor-last4=Sousa |editor-first4=Suseile Andrade}}</ref>

==Lexicon==
===[[Verbal number]]===
Some verbs form number pairs, whereby the choice of the verb depends on the number of the [[Ergative-absolutive alignment|absolutive]] participant (i.e., the subject of an intransitive verb or the [[Patient (linguistics)|patient]] of a transitive verb). The noun phrase which encodes the participant does not receive any overt marking.

==Vocabulary==
Maxakalí has a number of lexical loans from one of the [[Língua Geral]] varieties, such as ''ãmãnex'' ‘priest’, ''tãyũmak'' ‘money’, ''kãmãnok'' ‘horse’, ''tapayõg'' ‘Black man’.<ref name="Nikulin-Silva-Maxakalí-Krenák">{{Cite journal |last=Nikulin |first=Andrey |last2=Silva |first2=Mário André Coelho da |date=2020 |title=As línguas Maxakalí e Krenák dentro do tronco Macro-Jê |url=http://www.etnolinguistica.org/article:vol8n1-1 |journal=Cadernos de Etnolingüística |language=pt |volume=8 |issue=1 |pages=1–64}}</ref>

Loanwords from [[Brazilian Portuguese]] are extremely numerous. Examples include ''kapex'' ‘coffee’, ''komenok'' ‘blanket’, ''kapitõg'' ‘captain’, ''pẽyõg'' ‘beans’, ''mug'' ‘bank’, ''tenemiyam'' ‘TV’ (borrowed from Portuguese ''café'', ''cobertor'', ''capitão'', ''feijão'', ''banco'', ''televisão'').<ref name="Nikulin-Silva-Maxakalí-Krenák" /><ref>{{Cite journal |last=Ribeiro |first=Eduardo Rivail |date=2012 |title=Final consonants in Maxakalí and their comparative status |journal=LIAMES: Línguas Indígenas Americanas |language=en |volume=12 |issue=1 |pages=189–193 |doi=10.20396/liames.v0i12.1489 |doi-access=free}}</ref>
