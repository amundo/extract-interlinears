'''Mawayana''' (Mahuayana), also known as '''Mapidian''' (Maopidyán), is a moribund [[Arawakan language]] of northern South America. It is spoken by a dozen elderly people living in ethnic [[Wai-wai people|Wai-wai]] and [[Tiriyó people|Tiriyó]] villages in [[Brazil]] and [[Suriname]].<ref name="Meira-2019"/>

== Classification ==
{{harvcoltxt|Aikhenvald|1999}} lists Mawayana (and possibly Mawakwa as a dialect) together with [[Wapishana language|Wapishana]] under a Rio Branco (North-Arawak) branch of the Arawakan family. {{harvcoltxt|Carlin|2006|p= 314}} notes that Mawayana "is closely related to Wapishana" and according to {{harvcoltxt|Ramirez|2001|p= 530}} they share at least 47% of their lexicon.

== Phonology ==
Mawayana has, among its consonants, two [[implosive consonant|implosives]], {{IPA|/ɓ/}} and {{IPA|/ɗ/}}, and what has been described as a "retroflex fricativised rhotic", represented with {{angbr|rž}}, that it shares with [[Wapishana language|Wapishana]]. The vowel systems contains four vowels ({{IPA|/i-e, a, ɨ, u-o/}}), each of which has a nasalised counterpart.<ref>{{harvcoltxt|Carlin|2006|p= 320}}</ref>

== Morphosyntax ==
Mawayana has a polysynthetic morphology, mainly head-marking and with suffixes, although there are pronominal prefixes. The verbal arguments are indexed on the verb through subject suffixes on intransitive verbs, while agent prefixes and object suffixes on transitive verbs {{harvcol|Carlin|2006|page= 319}}.
