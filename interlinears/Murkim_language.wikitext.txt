'''Murkim''' is a [[Papuan language]] of [[Western New Guinea]], near its relatives [[Lepki language|Lepki]] and [[Kembra language|Kembra]]. Though spoken by fewer than 300 people, it is being learned by children. It is spoken in Murkim District, [[Pegunungan Bintang Regency]], Papua Province, Indonesia.

Dialects include the varieties spoken in Milki and Mot villages (Wambaliau 2004: 22-28).<ref name=ngw>[https://sites.google.com/site/newguineaworld/families/pauwasi-river/yetfa-south-pauwasi-river/south-pauwasi-river New Guinea World, South Pauwasi River]</ref>
