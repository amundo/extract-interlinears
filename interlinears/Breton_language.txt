Note that in the examples above the [[Goidelic languages]] (Irish, Scottish Gaelic, and Manx) use the preposition meaning ''at'' to show possession, whereas the [[Brittonic languages]] use ''with''. The Goidelic languages, however, do use the preposition ''with'' to express "belong to" (Irish langgais liom an leabhar, Scottish langgdis leam an leabhar, Manx langgvs'lhiams yn lioar, The book belongs to me).
