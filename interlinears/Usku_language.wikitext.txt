'''Usku''', or '''Afra''', is a nearly [[language death|extinct]] and poorly documented [[Papuan languages|Papuan language]] spoken by 20 or more people, mostly adults, in [[:id:Usku, Senggi, Keerom|Usku]] village, [[Senggi District]], [[Keerom Regency]], [[Papua (Indonesian province)|Papua]], [[Indonesia]].

Wurm (1975) placed it as an independent branch of [[Trans–New Guinea languages|Trans–New Guinea]], but [[Malcolm Ross (linguist)|Ross]] (2005) could not find enough evidence to classify it. Usher (2020) found that it was one of the [[West Pauwasi languages]], though divergent from the other two branches of that family.<ref>[https://sites.google.com/site/newguineaworld/families/pauwasi-river/west-pauwasi-river New Guinea World]</ref> Foley (2018) classifies Usku as a [[language isolate]].<ref name="Foley-NWNG"/>

An automated computational analysis ([[Automated Similarity Judgment Program|ASJP]] 4) by Müller et al. (2013)<ref name="ASJP-4">Müller, André, Viveka Velupillai, Søren Wichmann, Cecil H. Brown, Eric W. Holman, Sebastian Sauppe, Pamela Brown, Harald Hammarström, Oleg Belyaev, Johann-Mattis List, Dik Bakker, Dmitri Egorov, Matthias Urban, Robert Mailhammer, Matthew S. Dryer, Evgenia Korovina, David Beck, Helen Geyer, Pattie Epps, Anthony Grant, and Pilar Valenzuela. 2013. ''[https://asjp.clld.org/static/WorldLanguageTree-004.zip ASJP World Language Trees of Lexical Similarity: Version 4 (October 2013)]''.</ref> found lexical similarities between Usku and [[Kaure languages|Kaure]]. However, since the analysis was automatically generated, the grouping could be either due to mutual lexical borrowing or genetic inheritance.

==Morphology==
Usku morphology as inferred by Foley (2018):<ref name="Foley-NWNG"/>

*[[dative]] marker ''se''
*tense suffix ''-mu ~ -mo''
*[[allative]] [[postposition]] ''se''
*[[ablative]] ''e''

==Sentences==
Word order in Usku is SOV.<ref name="Foley-NWNG"/>

Some of the few documented sentences in Usku are:<ref name="Foley-NWNG"/>

{{interlinear|number=(1)
|e wang o ai se roti-mo
|3 money 1SG father DAT give-TNS
|‘She gave money to my father.’}}

{{interlinear|number=(2)
|e kompong se rifli-mo
|3 village DAT go-TNS
|‘He went to the village.’}}

{{interlinear|number=(3)
|kɨnmar kompong e duar-mo
|person village ABL come-TNS
|‘That person came from the village.’}}

{{interlinear|number=(4)
|kɨnmar mra-mu ya-mu
|person dog-ERG/FOC? bite-TNS
|‘The dog bit that person.’}}
