'''Moronene''' is an [[Austronesian language]] spoken in [[Bombana Regency]], [[Southeast Sulawesi]], [[Indonesia]]. It belongs to the [[Bungku–Tolaki languages|Bungku–Tolaki]] branch of the [[Celebic languages|Celebic]] subgroup.

The vowel phonemes are /a e i o u/. Sequences of two like vowels are pronounced as a long vowel, e.g. ''nee'' [ne:].<ref name=Andersen/>

== Grammar ==

=== Word Order ===
Moronene has flexible word order. However, there is a high frequency of clause-initial verbs in "connected narrative discourse." Noun phrases are not marked for case.<ref name=":0">Andersen, Suree. 1999. When the Moronene say no. In David Mead (ed.), Studies in Sulawesi linguistics part V, 73-112. Jakarta, Indonesia: Universitas Katolik Indonesia Atma Jaya.</ref>  The language has prepositions. 

=== Pronouns ===
There are two forms of pronouns, free pronouns and absolutive clitics.<ref name="Andersen" /> There are singular and plural forms, there are no dual, trial or paucal forms. There is an inclusive/exclusive distinction in the first person plural forms. There is no gender, and there appears to be no present-day politeness distinction.

=== Genitive Pronouns ===
There are two classes of genitive pronouns in Moronene which must be learned by speakers, which is unique among Bungku–Tolaki languages.<ref name=":1">Mead, David. 1998. ''Proto–Bungku-Tolaki: Reconstruction of its phonology and aspects of its morphosyntax''. PhD dissertation. Houston: Rice University.</ref> There are singular and plural forms, there are no dual, trial or paucal forms. There is an inclusive/exclusive distinction in the first person plural forms. Example (1) demonstrates the class 1 first person genitive pronoun in use with the noun 'hair'.<ref name=":1" />

=== Number ===
Moronene has a decimal numeral system.<ref>Andersen, T. David. 1999. Moronene numbers. In David Mead (ed.), Studies in Sulawesi linguistics part V, 1-72. Jakarta, Indonesia: Universitas Katolik Indonesia Atma Jaya.</ref>
