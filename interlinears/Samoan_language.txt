‘Ua lē fiafia le tama
PERF not happy ART boy
'The boy is unhappy.'

In this example of a negated [[Sentence (linguistics)declarative sentence]], it can be seen that, in Samoan, there is no equivalent [[Interlinear glossgloss]] for 'unhappy'. The negative particle ''lē'' modifies the verbal clause to form something like "not happy" instead.
