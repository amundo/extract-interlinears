'''Nomatsiguenga''' (Matsigenka) is an [[Arawakan language]] of [[Peru]]. It is close enough to [[Machiguenga language|Machiguenga]] to sometimes be considered dialects of a single language, especially given that both are spoken by the [[Machiguenga people]]. Most speakers are monolingual.

==Grammar==
Nomatsiguenga is one of the few languages in the world that has two different [[causative]] mechanisms to denote whether the causer was involved in the activity with the causee or not. The prefix ''ogi-'' is used to express the idea that the causer was ''not'' involved in the activity, while the suffix ''-hag'' is used when the causer ''is'' involved.<ref>Wise, M.R. (1986). "Grammatical characteristics of PreAndine Arawaken languages of Peru." pg. 567–642. In Derbyshire, D. C. & Pullum, G. K., eds. (1986). ''Handbook of Amazonian languages, Vol. 1'''. Berlin: Mouton de Gruyter. Cited in Dixon, R.M.W. (2000). "A Typology of Causatives: Form, Syntax, and Meaning". In Dixon, R.M.W. & Aikhenvald, Alexendra Y. Changing Valency: Case Studies in Transitivity. Cambridge University Press.</ref>
